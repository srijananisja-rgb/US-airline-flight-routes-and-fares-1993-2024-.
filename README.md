# U.S. Domestic Airfare & Air Travel Trends Analysis (1993–2024)

A Tableau-based data visualization project exploring 30+ years of U.S. domestic airline pricing, fuel costs, passenger demand, revenue composition, and on-time performance — built for Visual Analytics Project.

---

## Project Overview

This project uses Tableau to analyze how U.S. domestic air travel has evolved from 1993 to 2024. It combines multiple datasets covering airfares, jet fuel prices, passenger volumes, airline revenue breakdowns, and airport on-time performance to tell a data-driven story about pricing trends, route popularity, and the factors driving the cost of flying.

---

## Data Sources

- **US Airline Flight Routes and Fares (1993–2024)** — historical route-level fare and passenger data
- **3Q 2024 Air Fare Tables** — current quarter fare benchmarks
- **Airline Fares in U.C. City AVG** — city-level average fare data
- **Airport On-Time Performance** — on-time arrival/departure statistics by airport and year
- **Jet Fuel Price (FOB, $/gallon)** — historical jet fuel pricing
- **Airline Operating Revenue (Passenger Fares % of Total)** — revenue composition over time

---

## Key Analyses

### 1. Inflation-Adjusted Fare Trends
Compared **average domestic fares in current dollars vs. constant 2024 dollars** by quarter/year to reveal whether flying has actually gotten cheaper or more expensive in real terms over the past three decades.

### 2. Fuel Price vs. Fare Correlation
Analyzed the relationship between **jet fuel prices** and **average airfares** over time and by decade, to assess how much fuel costs influence ticket prices.

### 3. Passenger Volume & Route Popularity
Examined **average passenger counts by departure city** and identified the **most popular routes**, supported by origin/destination heatmaps and geographic maps of airfare by route.

### 4. Airline Revenue Composition
Tracked the **percentage of total airline operating revenue derived from passenger fares** over time, highlighting shifts toward ancillary revenue streams (baggage fees, etc.).

### 5. On-Time Performance Trends
Analyzed **on-time percentage by airport and year** to explore the relationship between service reliability and travel demand/route popularity.

### 6. Seasonal & Holiday Effects
Investigated how **fares and travel patterns shift around holiday periods**, captured in a dedicated holiday dashboard.

## Key Takeaways

- Domestic airfares show a measurable divergence between **current-dollar** and **inflation-adjusted** pricing, indicating real changes in travel affordability over time.
- **Jet fuel prices** show a meaningful relationship with fare fluctuations, particularly during periods of oil price volatility.
- A small set of routes and cities account for a disproportionate share of passenger volume, informing where airlines concentrate capacity.
- The share of airline revenue from **passenger fares alone** has shifted over time, reflecting broader industry trends toward ancillary fees.
- On-time performance varies significantly by airport and year, with potential links to demand and route congestion.
